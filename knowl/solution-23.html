Solution 3.1.9.1.

It might be informative to solve this directly from the definition, even though we have the convenient formula of LemmaÂ 3.1.6. Notice that \(E(X) = E(Y) = 1\text{.}\) Look at the joint mass function:
\begin{equation*} \begin {array} {c | ccc | c} X / Y \amp 0 \amp 1 \amp 2 \amp f_X \\\hline 0 \amp 1/8 \amp 1/8 \amp 0 \amp 1/4 \\ 1 \amp 1/8 \amp 1/4 \amp 1/8 \amp 1/2 \\ 2 \amp 0 \amp 1/8 \amp 1/8 \amp 1/4 \\\hline f_Y \amp 1/4 \amp 1/2 \amp 1/4 \amp \end {array} \end{equation*}
and compute the covariance directly from the definition:
\begin{align*} \cov(X,Y) \amp = E((X-EX)(Y-EY)) \\ \amp = \sum_{i,j} f_{X,Y}(x_i,y_j)(x_i-1)(y_j-1) \\ \amp = \frac18(0-1)(0-1) + \frac18(2-1)(2-1) = \frac14 \end{align*}
It is easier (and in more complicated examples this will be truer) to use the formula, though:
\begin{equation*} E(XY) = 1\frac28 + 2\frac18 + 2\frac18 + 4\frac18 = \frac54, \end{equation*}
so
\begin{equation*} \cov(X,Y) = E(XY) - E(X)E(Y) = \frac54 - 1 = \frac14. \end{equation*}
