The covariance of random variables \(X\) and \(Y\) is defined by
\begin{equation*} \cov(X,Y) = E((X - EX)(Y - EY)). \end{equation*}
The correlation coefficient of \(X\) and \(Y\) is
\begin{equation*} \rho(X,Y) = \frac{ \cov(X,Y)}{\sqrt{\var(X)}\sqrt{\var(Y)}}, \end{equation*}
provided \(\var(X)\) and \(\var(Y)\) are both nonzero.
