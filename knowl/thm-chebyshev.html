Theorem 2.4.1. Chebyshev’s Inequality.

Let \(X\) be a random variable and \(a \gt 0\text{.}\) Then
\begin{equation*} P( |X| \ge a) \le \frac{E(X^2)}{a^2}. \end{equation*}
In particular, when \(\mu = E(X) = 0\) and \(\var(X) = \sigma^2\text{,}\) we have
\begin{equation*} P(|X| \ge a) \le \frac{\sigma^2}{a^2}. \end{equation*}
So \(X\) must concentrate around its mean in a way that’s bounded by its variance.
