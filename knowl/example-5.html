Example 1.2.7.

Draw one card from a standard 52-card deck. Show that suit is independent of rank; for example, show that drawing a jack is independent of drawing a heart.

Solution.

The probability of drawing a jack is \(\tfrac{4}{52} = \tfrac{1}{13}\text{,}\) and the probability of drawing a heart is \(\tfrac{13}{52} = \tfrac14\text{.}\) The probability of drawing the Jack of Hearts is \(\tfrac{1}{52} = \tfrac{1}{13} \cdot \tfrac{1}{4}\text{.}\)
