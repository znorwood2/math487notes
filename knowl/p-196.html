  1. By Markov, \(P(X \ge 75) \le \frac{E(X)}{75} = \frac{50}{75} = \frac23\text{.}\)
  2. Apply Chebyshev to \(X - 50\text{,}\) which has the same variance as \(X\) but mean 0:
    \begin{equation*} P( |X - 50| \ge 10) \le \frac{E(X^2)}{10^2} = \frac{25}{100} = \frac14. \end{equation*}
