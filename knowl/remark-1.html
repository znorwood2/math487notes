Remark 1.2.5.

  • Notice that \(P(A\cap B) = P(A | B) P(B) = P(B | A) P(A)\text{.}\) So, as long as \(P(A) \gt 0\) and \(P(B) \gt 0\) the independence of \(A\) and \(B\) is equivalent to either \(P(A | B) = P(A)\) or \(P(B | A) = P(B)\text{;}\) that is, information about either event doesnâ€™t change the probability of the other.
  • Independence is not the same as disjointness; independent events \(A\) and \(B\) may or may not satisfy \(A \cap B = \emptyset\text{.}\)
