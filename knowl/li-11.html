Item.
Notice that \(P(A\cap B) = P(A | B) P(B) = P(B | A) P(A)\text{.}\) So, as long as \(P(A) \gt 0\) and \(P(B) \gt 0\) the independence of \(A\) and \(B\) is equivalent to either \(P(A | B) = P(A)\) or \(P(B | A) = P(B)\text{;}\) that is, information about either event doesn't change the probability of the other.
