Situation B. Now flip only one coin and consider \(X = 1_H\) and \(Y = 1_T\text{.}\) (That is, \(X\) is the indicator rv of the event that the result is a heads, and \(Y\) indicates whether the result is a tails.) Then \(X\) and \(Y\) are identically distributed \(\Bernoulli(1/2)\) random variables, just as in Situation A.
